Overview

StuCam is a mobile application designed to make it simple for clients to discover and book nearby photographers from students and beginners to aspiring professionals quickly, affordably, and with confidence.

The platform supports photographers at all experience levels by giving them opportunities to build their portfolio, gain real-world experience, earn income, and grow their reputation. At the same time, StuCam offers clients including students, creators, performers, and small businesses a trusted and accessible way to find affordable creative talent for projects, events, and personal content.

Through its clear profiles, transparent pricing, availability tools, and structured booking flow, StuCam aims to solve common challenges found in the photography ecosystem, where people often rely on unreliable DMs, word-of-mouth, or expensive platforms to find help. The result is a local, trust-driven marketplace that brings photographers and clients together in an organized, user-friendly experience.

The Problem

Finding a photographer today is surprisingly hard ‚Äî especially if you're on a student budget or need someone last-minute.

Clients rely on:

  • Instagram DMs
  • Word-of-mouth
  • Scrolling hashtags
  • Fiverr (often expensive and not local)

Meanwhile, beginner photographers struggle with:

  • Gaining credibility
  • Finding paying clients
  • Communicating expectations
  • Building real-world experience

There's no centralized, trustworthy, local-first platform where both sides can connect easily.

This gap is especially visible among students, small businesses, musicians, and early-career creatives.

Research

Goals

  • Understand how people currently find photographers
  • Explore challenges beginner photographers face
  • Identify trust signals clients rely on
  • Understand expectations before, during, and after a photoshoot
  • Discover opportunities for a more reliable booking flow

Methods

  • 1:1 interviews with clients and photographers
  • Competitor analysis (Fiverr, Instagram, GigSalad, Snappr, etc.)
  • Journey mapping
  • Pain point clustering
  • Behavior and motivation analysis

Key Insights

1. Trust is the foundation.

People won't book without:

  • A portfolio
  • Reviews
  • Proof of identity
  • Clear communication

2. Availability often matters more than perfection.

For last-minute gigs, the biggest question is:

"Is anyone available right now or this weekend?"

3. Pricing needs to be transparent.

Clients dislike negotiating through DMs ‚Äî they want clear, upfront pricing.

4. Beginner photographers need structure.

They crave guidance on:

  • Pricing
  • Preparing for a shoot
  • Setting boundaries
  • Communicating with clients

5. Both sides experience frustration with DM-based booking.

Instagram DMs lack structure, professionalism, and organization.

Design Goals

  • Make local discovery fast and intuitive
  • Showcase portfolios and reviews to build trust
  • Create a clean booking flow that works in minutes
  • Let photographers control their availability and pricing
  • Support photographers' professional growth
  • Keep the UI simple, modern, and student-friendly

Solution: StuCam

StuCam brings clients and photographers together through a role-based, one-app experience.

Key Features for Clients

  • Browse nearby photographers through a clean list (future: map)
  • Apply filters: style, price, availability, distance, rating
  • View detailed profiles with curated portfolios
  • Book instantly with a sticky Book Now button
  • Message photographers for logistics
  • Track bookings: Pending, Upcoming, Completed
  • Review photographers after shoots

Key Features for Photographers

  • Build profile + portfolio
  • Set hourly rates
  • Mark availability with toggle
  • Accept/decline bookings
  • Manage schedule
  • Chat with clients
  • Share work via Feed: Following ¬∑ Trending ¬∑ Near You
  • Build reputation with reviews

Iterations

Initially: ZipyHelp

Too broad ‚Üí hard to design deeply.

Pivot: StuCam

Focused domain ‚Üí deeper research + better UX.

Other Key Decisions:

  • One app, role-based UI
  • Feed tabs: Following / Trending / Near You
  • Portfolio = curated work; Posts = lighter activity preview
  • Prioritized trust signals in profile
  • Booking-first message inbox

What I Learned

  • Focusing the scope improves both research and design quality
  • Beginners need professional structure
  • Trust is the "currency" of marketplaces
  • Locality and availability significantly shape UX
  • Building in Cursor/Vibe coding accelerated prototyping
  • Feedback loops dramatically improve flows
